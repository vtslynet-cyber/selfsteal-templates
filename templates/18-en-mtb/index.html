02 June

South Spur after the rain

Whistler, BC · 47 km · 1850 m elevation

Two days of rain and the lower berms turned to glass. The descent from the col is technical at the best of times — wet roots, a few committing rock rolls, and the long off-camber traverse before the meadow. We took it slow.

Tested the new tyre setup (2.5" front / 2.4" rear, 18 / 21 psi). Held line better than expected through the loam, but the rear was sketchy on the off-camber.

Distance 47 km Climb 1850 m Time 5h 22m
26 May

Frame refresh: bearings, bolts, paint chips

Workshop notes · 3 hr

Pulled the rear pivot bearings — 14 months and they were still smooth, but the dust seals were dying. Full service: new bearings, fresh grease, torqued to spec.

Touched up paint chips with two-pack auto enamel. Not invisible, but it'll see another season without rust creeping in.

18 May

Long route through the Coast Range

Squamish to Pemberton · 3 days · self-supported

Bivvied above the third lake on night one. Wind shifted at three in the morning and we spent the next hour repacking the tarp.

Day two was the prize — twenty kilometres of ridge with nothing but marmots and granite.
